Our Story

How we met

Our lives first crossed paths just over 2 years ago (September 2013). Rachel was casually dating Eddie’s good friend and boss at the time, and we were introduced by him over a few games of pool. We started talking more over the phone when Eddie’s boss asked Rachel to “set up” Eddie with one of her single friends. We slowly got to know each other better in an effort to change Eddie’s “single” status, but we didn’t envision that the real connection would eventually be between us!

Shortly after we started to talk, we set up a night out and Eddie came over to Rachel’s house first to have dinner and meet her roommate and a few female friends. Long story short, Rachel drank way too much and the night out ended rather abruptly (after some chivalrous hair-holding efforts by Eddie). Following the less than romantic end to the evening, Rachel and Eddie’s boss decided to just be friends. However, Eddie texted Rachel early the next morning and asked to hang out again (Eddie claims he was smitten immediately, before Rachel even realized it). As they say, the rest is history! We’ve been basically inseparable ever since!

The proposal

It was our 2-year anniversary (October 13th, 2015) and I (Rachel) was getting ready for work. It was 5:30am and Eddie’s son, Elijah, was with us at the time, still sleeping in his room. Eddie was out of bed IMMEDIATELY when his alarm went off, which I thought was strange because he normally requires several alarms before he even acknowledges that it is morning. When he heard Elijah stir, he went in to get him and came into the bathroom where I was getting ready. Without pause, Eddie was down on one knee with the ring in the box in his hand. Elijah, whom he still held in his arms, also held an identical ring box with a candy Ring Pop, and he held it up to me as Eddie asked me if I would marry him. I was utterly surprised and dumbfounded but managed to say, “Yes, of course!” It was so private, simple, and precious and involved Elijah, whom I love with all my heart! It couldn’t have been more perfect.
